FAQ

Questions & answers

Everything about downloading from Pinterest with Revapin — safety, formats, pricing, and the odd hiccup.

Getting started
What is Revapin?

A Chrome extension that saves pins, whole boards, and videos from Pinterest straight to your computer — right from the page you're looking at, as a ZIP, a folder, or a PDF.

Is it free?

Yes to start. The free plan covers 25 pin saves and 3 board downloads a month, in HD, with no card required. If you download a lot, the paid plans lift the limits from $3.99.

How do I install it?

Add it from the Chrome Web Store, then open any board on Pinterest — the toolbar appears on the page. There's a 2-minute setup guide if you want the details.

Which browsers does it work in?

Chrome and any Chromium-based browser: Microsoft Edge, Brave, Arc, and Opera all work the same way.

Do I need to create an account?

No Revapin account, no sign-up, no configuration. You just need to be logged into your own Pinterest so it can see your boards.

Downloading
How do I download a whole board?

Open the board, tick the pins you want or use "Whole board" to grab all of them, then click Download and pick a format. The files land in your Downloads folder.

What formats can I save in?

Three: a ZIP archive, a plain folder of image files, or a single PDF moodboard you can send to a client.

Can I download videos?

Yes. Video pins are saved as ready-to-use MP4 files in full quality, so you can reuse them straight away.

Do GIFs and idea pins work?

GIFs come down as animated files, and idea pins are pulled page by page, so you keep each part of the sequence.

Are the images full resolution?

Yes. Revapin fetches the original file each pin was uploaded at, not the shrunken preview you see in the feed — good enough to print or drop into a design.

Can I grab a single pin?

Yes. A download button sits on every pin, so you can save just one instantly, even while other downloads are running.

Can I download whole profiles and sections?

Yes. You can pull a creator's entire profile, and each board section comes down as its own folder, so the structure stays intact.

How many pins can it handle at once?

Whole boards and full profiles. Large selections are split into several ZIPs automatically so your browser never runs out of memory, and you can run more than one download queue at the same time.

Will re-downloading overwrite my files?

No. Each download creates a fresh folder and skips pins you've already saved, so nothing is ever overwritten.

Privacy & safety
Does it work on private and secret boards?

Yes. It runs inside your own logged-in Pinterest, so any board you can open — secret boards and ones shared with you included — you can save. Boards no one shared with you stay out of reach.

Will using it get my Pinterest account banned?

No. Revapin never logs in for you, posts, follows, or automates anything on your account — it only saves the pins you're already viewing. There's no password to hand over and nothing is sent to a server, so there's nothing for Pinterest to flag.

Does it upload my data anywhere?

No. Everything runs locally in your own browser. Nothing about your boards or your account leaves your computer.

What data does it collect?

Nothing personal. Your boards, pins, and account details are never collected or sent anywhere.

Can it access my Pinterest account?

It only reads the page you're looking at, the same content you can already see. It doesn't touch your settings, messages, or anything behind your login.

Troubleshooting
The toolbar isn't showing up

Refresh the Pinterest tab. A page that was already open before you installed the extension won't have the toolbar until it reloads.

A download stopped or didn't finish

Hit Stop to cancel — any unfinished pins stay selected so you can try again, and files saved so far are kept. For a big board, downloads split into several archives, so give it a moment to finish each part.

I can't find the downloaded files

They save to your browser's normal Downloads folder. Check your download location in the browser settings if you've changed it from the default.

Is downloading from Pinterest allowed?

Saving pins for your own reference and moodboards is normal, and everything stays on your computer. The images still belong to their creators, so keep them for personal use and credit people when you share. There's a fuller explainer here.
