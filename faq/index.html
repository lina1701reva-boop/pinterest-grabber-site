FAQ

Everything you'd want to know before installing

The honest answers — why it beats saving pins by hand, whether it's safe for your account, and what it costs.

Is it better than doing it by hand?
Why not just right-click and save the image?

Right-click gives you whatever size Pinterest shows on screen — usually a shrunk preview — one pin at a time, with a messy filename to fix. Revapin pulls the original full-resolution file, takes a whole board at once, and drops it into a tidy folder, ZIP, or PDF. Past a couple of pins, that's two minutes instead of an evening.

How is this different from the free online downloaders?

Those make you copy a board link into a site you don't control, one board at a time, often at low quality and sometimes with your data passing through their server. Revapin runs inside your own browser, right on the page, with nothing uploaded anywhere — plus folders, ZIPs, PDF moodboards, and full-resolution files those tools rarely match.

Will it do what I need?
Can I grab a whole board — or a whole profile — in one go?

Yes. One action takes a full board, keeps each section as its own folder, and pulls a creator's entire profile the same way. Even a huge board is fine: it splits into several ZIPs automatically so your browser never runs out of memory, and you can run downloads in parallel.

Are the images and videos full quality, or the blurry previews?

Full quality. Revapin fetches the original file each pin was uploaded at — sharp enough to print or drop into a design — and saves videos as ready-to-use MP4s. It sorts photos and videos for you, so you can pull only what you want.

Can I turn a board into something I'd actually send a client?

Yes — export any board or section as a single PDF moodboard. It reads as far more finished than sending a Pinterest link, and it's one of the main reasons designers keep Revapin around.

Does it work on my private and secret boards?

Yes. It runs inside your own logged-in Pinterest, so any board you can open — secret boards and ones shared with you included — you can save. Nothing you can't already see, and nothing you can.

Picking exactly what you want
Can I choose just a few pins instead of the whole board?

Yes. A little circle appears on every pin — tick the ones you want. Or drag a box across several pins at once to grab them all in one go (hold Alt/Option and drag back over them to un-tick). You can select one pin, a handful, or everything on the page.

Can I download only the photos, or only the videos?

Yes. One click filters a board to just its photos, or just its videos, so you save exactly the type you want and skip the rest. Photos and videos are also kept in separate folders automatically.

Can I take a whole board, a single section, or my entire profile?

All three. Grab one full board, pick just the sections you care about (or all of them at once), or download every board on a profile in a single action. Each section stays in its own folder so nothing gets mixed up.

What about carousel pins that have several images?

When you pick a carousel (a pin with multiple images), Revapin grabs every image inside it — all of them, not just the cover — and the counter shows you how many it found.

Formats and how your files are saved
What formats can I download in?

Three: a ZIP archive, a plain folder of files, or a PDF moodboard. When you take several boards at once you can also choose one ZIP per board, one folder per board, or one ZIP per section — whatever's tidiest for you.

How are the files organized when they land?

Neatly, so you're not left with one giant pile. You get one folder (or ZIP) per board, a sub-folder for each section, photos and videos sorted into their own folders, and any loose pins in a "pins" folder — all named for you, in full original quality.

I have a huge board with thousands of pins — will I wait forever and get nothing?

No. Big boards are broken into parts of 500 files and saved section by section, so files start arriving right away instead of everything at the very end. You see live progress and the time left, you can stop whenever you like, and anything already saved stays put — with a one-click option to grab the rest later.

Is it safe?
Where do my pins actually go — is anything uploaded?

Straight into your own Downloads folder, and nowhere else. Everything runs locally in your browser; nothing about your boards or account ever leaves your computer.

Is downloading from Pinterest allowed?

Saving pins for your own reference and moodboards is normal, and it all stays on your machine. The images belong to their creators, so keep them for personal use and credit people when you share. There's a fuller explainer here.
