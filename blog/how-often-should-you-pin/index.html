How Often Should You Pin? A Realistic Posting Schedule

A handful of pins a day, kept up over months, does more than a big burst once a week. There's no magic number, and the old advice about pinning dozens of times daily is long gone. What Pinterest rewards now is steadiness and fresh content, at a pace you can actually maintain.

A number to start from

Somewhere between one and ten pins a day works for most accounts, weighted toward your own fresh pins rather than re-saving other people's. If you're new, start low — a couple a day you can sustain — and build up. A schedule you abandon in three weeks is worse than a smaller one you keep for a year.

Fresh content matters more than raw count

Pinterest favours fresh pins: new images, not the same one re-saved over and over. So the useful question isn't just how many times you pin, but how much new material you're adding. A few genuinely new pins beat twenty repeats of last month's.

Make it sustainable

The way to keep a pace is to remove the daily effort:

  • batch a week or month of pins in one sitting;
  • schedule them to post on a steady rhythm;
  • keep a folder of images ready so you're never starting from nothing.

Keep a library ready

Consistency is far easier when your raw material is already on your computer, sorted and waiting. Revapin downloads reference pins and your own boards into folders, so batching a week of content is a matter of pulling from files you already have rather than scrounging for images each time.
