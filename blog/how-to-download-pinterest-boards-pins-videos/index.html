How to Download Pinterest Boards, Pins & Videos (2026 Guide)

Pinterest is great for collecting ideas, but it keeps everything locked inside the app. If you want your pins as real files — to use offline, drop into a moodboard, or keep before a pin disappears — here is every way to get them out, from the slow manual method to whole boards in one click.

Why download from Pinterest at all?

A few common reasons people want their pins as files:

  • Building a moodboard for a client or a project, without an internet connection.
  • Keeping references before a pin gets deleted or the original link dies.
  • Organizing inspiration into folders on your own computer.
  • Backing up a board you spent months putting together.

The manual way (and why it's slow)

You can right-click any pin and choose “Save image as”. That works for one or two pins, but it falls apart fast: you get whatever size Pinterest shows on screen (often a smaller preview), you have to click through every single pin, and there's no way to grab a whole board at once. For a board with 200 pins, that's 200 right-clicks — and you still end up with mixed file sizes and messy names.

The fast way: a browser extension

A browser extension runs inside Pinterest and lets you tick the pins you want, then download them together. This is what Revapin does. Here's the basic flow:

  1. Install the extension and open any board in your browser.
  2. Tick the pins you want, or hit “Whole board” to grab all of them.
  3. Click Download and choose a ZIP, a folder, or a PDF.
  4. The files land in your Downloads folder at full resolution.

Everything happens in your browser, so you don't paste links into a random website or upload anything.

Downloading videos and idea pins

Video pins are trickier to save by hand because there's no obvious “save” button. A good extension pulls the real video file and saves it as a ready-to-use MP4 in full quality, so you can actually reuse it as a reference. Revapin handles video pins the same way as images — tick and download.

Whole boards, sections, and profiles

If you organize a board into sections, you probably want them to stay organized when you download. Look for a tool that can:

  • Download an entire board in one go.
  • Save each section as its own folder, instead of one messy pile.
  • Grab a creator's whole profile when you want everything.

For big boards, a well-built tool splits the download into several archives automatically so your browser doesn't run out of memory.

Get the original quality, not a thumbnail

The single biggest mistake when saving from Pinterest is ending up with blurry previews. The image you see on the board is usually smaller than the original. Make sure whatever method you use pulls the full-resolution file — otherwise your moodboard looks soft when you zoom in or print it.

A quick word on copyright

Downloading for your own reference and inspiration is normal, but the images still belong to their creators. Only reuse content you have the right to use, credit creators where you can, and don't republish someone else's work as your own.
