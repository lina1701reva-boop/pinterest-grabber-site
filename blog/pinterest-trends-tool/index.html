What Is Pinterest Trends and How to Use It for Content Ideas

Pinterest Trends is a free tool from Pinterest that shows you what people are searching for and how that search volume moves through the year. Type in a keyword and you get a chart of its popularity plus related terms, which tells you what to make and when to post it.

Where to find it and what it shows

Go to trends.pinterest.com and search any term. The tool draws a line showing how often people looked for that phrase over the past year or two, broken down by country. You'll spot the seasonal spikes right away — "fall outfits" climbs in August, "gingerbread house" peaks in early December, "meal prep" jumps every January.

Reading a rising search

A flat line means steady demand you can serve any time. A line bending upward means a search is heating up, and that's where the opportunity sits. Pinterest also shows related and trending terms below the chart, so one search opens into ten more ideas. Three things to pull from every search:

  • the exact phrasing, since that's what people type;
  • the month the search peaks each year;
  • the related terms listed below the chart.

Timing beats guessing

Pins take weeks to build momentum in search, so you post ahead of the peak, not during it. If Trends shows a search climbing in October and cresting in December, you publish in early November. Working back from the spike is the whole game.

Once Trends points you at a rising search, look at the pins already ranking for it.
