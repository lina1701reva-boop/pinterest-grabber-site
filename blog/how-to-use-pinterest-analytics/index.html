How to Use Pinterest Analytics to Improve Your Strategy

Pinterest Analytics tells you which of your pins are working and why. Focus on three numbers — impressions, saves, and outbound clicks — find the pins that score highest, and make more pins like them. That loop is the whole strategy.

The three numbers that matter

Impressions show how often a pin appeared. Saves show how many people wanted to keep it, which is Pinterest's strongest signal of a good pin. Outbound clicks show how many people went to your site. A pin with lots of impressions but few saves has a reach problem solved; it's the image or hook that's falling flat.

Find your top performers

Sort your pins by saves or by clicks in the Analytics tab. The ones at the top are telling you what your audience wants. Look for the pattern — a certain image style, a certain topic, a certain kind of headline. That pattern is worth repeating.

Do more of what works

Once you know your winners, the move is simple: make variations. Same topic, fresh image. Same layout, new photo. Pinterest rewards fresh pins, so remaking a proven idea with a new graphic gives you another strong pin without reinventing anything. A quick checklist each month:

  • note the three pins with the most saves;
  • make two or three fresh variations of each;
  • drop the formats that never gained traction.
