Pinterest Ads 101: How to Run Your First Campaign

Pinterest ads are just regular pins you pay to show to more people. You pick a goal, choose who sees the pin, set a daily budget, and Pinterest places it in feeds and search. The setup takes minutes; the skill is knowing which pin to promote and to whom.

How a campaign is built

You start in Pinterest Ads Manager. A campaign holds your overall goal — more clicks, more awareness, more conversions. Inside it you set the audience and budget, then choose an existing pin to promote. Pinterest bills you when people click or when the pin is shown, depending on the goal you picked.

Targeting and budget

You can target by keyword, by interest, or by an audience you upload, like your email list. Keyword targeting fits Pinterest best, since people are already searching. Start small — a few dollars a day is enough to learn what happens. Watch the cost per click and let a pin run long enough to gather real numbers before you judge it.

When ads make sense

Ads work when you already have a pin proving itself organically and you want to speed up its reach. They also help around a time-sensitive push, like a launch or a seasonal sale. Ads don't fix a weak pin or a broken landing page — they just buy more eyes for whatever you point them at.

Study organic top pins before paying

Before you spend a cent, look at the pins winning your keyword for free. A download extension lets you keep that top set on your computer to study the format, crops, and copy. Revapin saves those organic winners at full quality, so you promote a pin built the way the best ones are built, not a guess. A few things belong in every first campaign:

  • a proven pin, not a brand-new untested one;
  • keyword targeting that matches real searches;
  • a small daily budget you can watch and adjust.
