8 Pinterest Tips & Tricks for Designers and Creators

Most people treat Pinterest like a scrapbook. If you design, illustrate, plan events, or make content, a few small habits turn it into a real work tool. Here are eight that make a difference.

1. Split boards into sections

A board with 300 mixed pins is impossible to search later. Break each board into sections — “color”, “layout”, “typography”, “textures” — right from the start. Future you will find the exact reference in seconds instead of scrolling forever.

2. Use secret boards for client work

Keep work-in-progress and client ideas on secret boards. They don't show up on your public profile, so you can gather rough references without anyone seeing the messy early stage of a project.

3. Follow topics, not just people

Following individual accounts gives you a narrow feed. Following topics (and using the “More ideas” button on a board) feeds you fresh material around a theme, which is far more useful when you're hunting for something specific.

4. Learn the search filters

After you search, use the filter chips at the top to narrow by style, color, or type. Searching “kitchen” and then filtering to a single color is the fastest way to build a tight, on-brief board.

5. Turn a board into a moodboard you can share

Clients rarely want a Pinterest link — they want a clean document. Export a whole board as a single PDF and you have a moodboard you can attach to an email or drop into a deck. (Revapin does this in one click.)

6. Save reels and video pins for reference

Motion references are gold for animators and content creators, but Pinterest makes videos hard to keep. Save the ones you like as MP4 files so you can study them frame by frame later, offline.

7. Batch your references before a project starts

Instead of jumping back to Pinterest every five minutes while you work, spend ten minutes up front collecting everything into a board, then download the whole thing to a folder. Work from your own files and stay out of the endless scroll.

8. Back up the boards you actually care about

Pins disappear. Accounts get deleted. Links rot. If a board is important to your work, keep a local copy so a single deleted pin doesn't take your reference with it.
