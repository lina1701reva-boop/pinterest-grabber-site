How to Get More Followers on Pinterest

Followers count for less on Pinterest than almost anywhere else, because most of your reach comes from search rather than your follower feed. Growing them still helps, but the fastest way to more followers is to make pins that get found, and let the follows follow from there.

Get found before you get followed

Someone follows you after a pin of yours already caught them in search. So the groundwork is the same as ranking well: clear keywords in titles, descriptions, and board names, and images worth saving. Do that and you're putting your work in front of the people most likely to follow.

Give a profile worth following

When someone lands on your profile, a few things decide whether they stick around:

  • a clear bio that says what you're about;
  • boards named plainly, on topics you actually cover;
  • a consistent look across your pins, so the profile feels coherent.

A tidy, on-theme profile converts far better than a random pile of boards.

Show up steadily

Regular pinning beats occasional bursts. You don't need to flood the feed, but a steady trickle keeps you in circulation and gives the algorithm fresh material to test. Consistency over months does more for follower growth than any single viral pin.

Learn from accounts that grew

Profiles in your niche that already have a following are worth studying — their pin style, their board structure, the topics that repeat.
