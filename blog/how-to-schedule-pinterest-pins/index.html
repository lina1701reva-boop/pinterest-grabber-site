How to Schedule Pins in Advance on Pinterest

Consistency matters on Pinterest, but pinning by hand every day gets old fast. Scheduling lets you set pins up once and have them post over the coming days or weeks, so you stay active without living in the app. Pinterest has a scheduler built in, and there are third-party tools for bigger workflows.

Using the built-in scheduler

When you create a pin, instead of publishing straight away you can choose a date and time to publish it, up to a couple of weeks out. It's free and it's enough for most people. The limit is that you schedule one pin at a time and only so far ahead, which starts to feel slow at volume.

When a third-party tool helps

Dedicated schedulers add things the native tool doesn't:

  • queueing weeks or months of pins at once;
  • suggested posting times based on your audience;
  • bulk uploading a whole batch in one go.

They cost money and add a step, so they earn their place once you're pinning at real scale, not before.

Batch instead of drip

Whichever tool you use, the win is batching. Set aside one session to design and schedule a month of pins, and you're done thinking about it daily. A content calendar helps here — plan themes ahead, especially for anything seasonal, since Pinterest rewards pinning seasonal content weeks early.

Keep your pin library on hand

Batching is easier when your images are already sorted on your own computer rather than scattered across the web. Revapin downloads reference pins and your own boards into folders, so building a month of content means pulling from files you already have instead of hunting each time.
