Best Times to Post on Pinterest for Maximum Reach

Timing matters on Pinterest, but less than people expect. Because most reach comes from search over weeks, not a feed in the first hour, a pin posted at a quiet time still surfaces later. Posting when your audience is active gives an early nudge; it doesn't make or break a pin.

The general pattern

Across a lot of accounts, evenings and weekends tend to see more activity, when people have time to plan and browse. That's a starting point, not a rule. Treat any "best time" chart as a rough default until you have data from your own audience, which will usually differ.

Find your own best times

Your account's analytics show when your audience is actually online and engaging. Use that instead of a generic chart:

  • check which days and hours your pins get the most saves;
  • note the time zones your audience sits in;
  • test a few slots and keep the ones that perform.

Consistency beats perfect timing

Pinning steadily matters far more than nailing the ideal hour. A scheduler lets you spread pins across good slots without being at your desk, so you post at sensible times on autopilot. Set it and let the rhythm do the work; there's a guide to scheduling if you want the details.

Plan seasonal content early

The one timing rule that genuinely counts is being early for seasons. Pinterest users plan weeks ahead, so holiday and seasonal pins want to go up 30 to 60 days before the date.
