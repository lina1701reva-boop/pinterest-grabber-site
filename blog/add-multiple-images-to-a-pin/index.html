How to Add Multiple Images to a Single Pin

You can put more than one image in a single Pinterest pin using either a carousel or an Idea pin. Both let a viewer swipe through several photos in one place, which is handy for steps, variations, or a before-and-after. Which one you pick depends on whether you want a link and how much text you need.

Carousel pins

A carousel holds up to five images in one standard pin, and viewers swipe sideways to move between them. Every image can share the same title, description, and destination link, so a carousel works well when you're showing angles of one product or a short sequence that all points to the same page. You build it from the pin creation screen by adding several images before you publish.

Idea pins with multiple pages

Idea pins let you stack multiple images and video clips as pages, closer to a mini story. They're built for tutorials and multi-step content — recipes, room makeovers, packing lists. They don't carry an outbound link the way a carousel does, so use them when the goal is to teach or showcase rather than send traffic off Pinterest.

Choosing between them

Match the format to what you're trying to do:

  • carousel when you want a shared link across a few images;
  • Idea pin when you're walking through steps on-platform;
  • single pin when one strong image says it all.

Save multi-image references

When you find a carousel or Idea pin full of references you want to keep, screenshotting each frame is slow and lossy. A browser extension can pull the images down at full quality in one go; Revapin downloads pins and boards to your computer so a multi-image reference stays intact on your own drive.
