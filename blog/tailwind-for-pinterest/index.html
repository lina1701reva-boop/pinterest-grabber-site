How to Use Tailwind for Pinterest Scheduling

Tailwind is a Pinterest-approved scheduler that lets you load up pins and have them publish automatically over days or weeks. You add pins to a queue, and Tailwind spaces them out at times it thinks your audience is active. Here's how it works and when it's worth paying for.

What Tailwind actually is

Tailwind is a third-party tool that Pinterest has approved as an official partner. That approval matters. It connects through Pinterest's API, so the pins it publishes count as normal activity and won't get your account flagged the way a random bot would. You can schedule to Pinterest and Instagram from the same dashboard, but Pinterest is where most people start with it.

The queue and smart schedule

The core idea is the queue. Instead of picking an exact time for every pin, you drop pins into a list, and Tailwind fills preset time slots automatically. Its "smart schedule" suggests slots based on when your followers tend to be online, so you're not guessing. You can build a week of pins in one sitting, then let it drip them out.

A couple of features do real work here. Board Lists let you send one pin to a group of related boards on a spaced-out schedule, so it doesn't look like you dumped the same image everywhere at once. Interval pinning handles that spacing for you.

  • Load a batch of pins once, publish them across the whole week.
  • Let smart slots pick posting times instead of setting each one.
  • Send a pin to several boards on a stagger with Board Lists.

When it beats the native scheduler

Pinterest's own scheduler is free and fine for light use, but it caps you at two weeks out and one pin at a time, and you can't schedule the same pin to multiple boards. Tailwind costs money on a monthly or annual plan. If you're posting a handful of pins a week, the free native tool covers you. Once you're managing dozens of pins across many boards and the manual scheduling eats an hour a day, the paid plan starts paying for itself in time saved.

Keep a folder of images ready to load

Any scheduler is only as good as what you feed it. Sitting down to fill a week's queue goes fast when your images are already collected in one place, and drags when you're hunting through folders and old boards. A tool that pulls images down to your computer lets you stage a library ahead of time. Revapin is a Chrome extension that downloads pins, boards, and videos straight to your machine, so you can keep a ready folder of your own images to drag into Tailwind whenever you sit down to schedule.
