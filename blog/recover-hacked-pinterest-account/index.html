How to Recover a Hacked or Locked Pinterest Account

If your Pinterest account is hacked or locked, move fast: reset the password, secure the email tied to it, contact Pinterest support if you're shut out, and turn on two-factor so it can't happen again.

Reset the password right away

Start with the password reset. If you can still get in, change it immediately to something long and unique that you don't use anywhere else. If you're locked out, use the "forgot password" flow to send a reset link to your email. Getting a fresh password in place is what kicks whoever's in there back out, so do it before anything else.

Secure the email behind the account

Your Pinterest login is only as safe as the email attached to it. If an attacker controls that inbox, they can just reset the password again and take it back. So check the email account too: change its password, confirm the recovery details still point to you, and look for any forwarding rules or unfamiliar logins that shouldn't be there. Lock down the email first and the Pinterest recovery actually sticks.

Contact Pinterest support

If the attacker changed the email on the account, or the reset link never arrives, the self-serve route won't cut it and you'll need Pinterest support. Reach out through their help channels, explain that the account was compromised, and be ready to prove it's yours. This part can be slow, so start it early rather than after you've exhausted everything else. Watch out for fake "support" accounts and phishing messages while you're at it; go through Pinterest's official help, not a link someone DMs you.

Turn on two-factor, and back up so a lockout isn't a loss

Once you're back in, add two-factor authentication so a stolen password alone can't get anyone in. Then think about the deeper risk: an account can be lost for reasons that have nothing to do with a hack — a mistaken suspension, a support delay, a recovery that just doesn't work. If everything you've collected lives only on Pinterest, a bad week can wipe it out. Keeping a copy on your own machine takes that fear off the table. A download extension makes it painless. Revapin saves your boards, or your whole profile, to your computer as folders or a ZIP, so a lockout means a hassle, not the loss of years of pins.

The order that works best:

  • reset the Pinterest password, then secure the linked email;
  • contact official support if you're fully locked out;
  • turn on two-factor and keep a local backup of your boards.

Recovering an account is stressful mostly because of what's riding on it. Get the login back, close the door behind you, and keep a copy of your collection somewhere Pinterest can't reach — so next time it's an inconvenience instead of a disaster.
