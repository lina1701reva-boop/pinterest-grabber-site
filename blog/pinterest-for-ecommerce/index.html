Pinterest for E-commerce: How to Turn Pins into Sales

Shoppers come to Pinterest already planning purchases, which puts an online store in front of people at the right moment. A pin can carry a live price and link straight to the product page, so the path from seeing an item to buying it is short. Set up well, it becomes a sales channel that keeps working on its own.

Turn on product pins

Product rich pins pull the current price and availability from your store onto the pin, and keep them in sync. A shopper sees the real price before clicking, which filters for genuine interest and reads as trustworthy. Most store platforms expose the right metadata already, so switching this on is mostly a one-time setup.

Show products the way people picture them

Plain catalogue shots convert worse than products shown in context:

  • the item styled or worn, in a real setting;
  • a few angles or variations across separate pins;
  • seasonal versions timed a month ahead.

Each pin is another way into the same product page, and lifestyle images help a shopper imagine owning the thing.

Send clicks to the exact product

Link each pin to the specific product it shows, not a category page or the homepage. The shopper tapped because of that item; dropping them anywhere else adds friction and loses the sale. Match the pin, the link, and the landing page so arriving feels seamless.

Model your pins on what already sells

The product pins ranking for your category are a live guide to what earns clicks and saves. Saving a set of them lets you study the styling, crops, and text that convert in your space. Revapin downloads them to your computer so you can keep a reference file and design product pins that compete instead of guessing at it.

Enable product pins, show items in context across several images, and link each one to its exact page. Do that steadily and Pinterest becomes a storefront that keeps sending buyers well past the day you posted.
