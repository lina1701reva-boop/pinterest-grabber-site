Pinterest for Wedding and Event Planning Businesses

Couples plan weddings on Pinterest before they book a single vendor. That makes it the room where your future clients already are, saving colours, table settings, and dress ideas for the day they've imagined. Show up there well and you catch them while the plans are still forming.

Showcase real events you've planned

Pin photos from weddings and events you've run, and link them to your portfolio or contact page. A couple who saves your tablescape wants to know who made it, so give the pin somewhere to go. Group your work into boards by style, season, or venue so someone can picture how you'd handle their day.

Run secret boards for each client

Secret boards are only visible to you and anyone you invite, which makes them a clean way to plan a specific event with a client. Set one up per couple, invite them, and pin ideas back and forth. You keep the planning private, they see it come together in one place, and nothing shows up on your public profile until you want it to.

Build moodboards clients can react to

A shared board becomes a living moodboard for the event. Pin options and let the couple weigh in before anything is booked. It works well for pinning down the pieces early:

  • colour palette and floral direction;
  • table settings, stationery, and signage style;
  • venue mood and lighting references.

Seeing it laid out together helps a couple commit, and it cuts the back-and-forth once the plan is set.

Turn a board into a PDF moodboard for clients

Sometimes a client wants the moodboard as a file they can hold onto, print, or forward to a partner who isn't on Pinterest. A download extension like Revapin exports a whole board to a single PDF, so you can hand a couple a tidy moodboard of their event, or keep an offline copy of a secret board once the day is done.
