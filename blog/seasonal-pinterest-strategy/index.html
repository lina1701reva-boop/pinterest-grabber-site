Seasonal Pinning Strategy: Planning 45–90 Days Ahead

On Pinterest, seasonal content works only if it's early. People plan holidays and events weeks in advance, so your seasonal pins should go up 45 to 90 days before the date, not the week of. Pin your Christmas ideas in October, and reuse the strong ones every year after.

Why early wins

Pinterest is where people plan, not where they act on the day. Someone searching "Thanksgiving table" is looking in September and October, choosing ideas to save for later. If your pin appears the week of the holiday, that planning is already done and you've missed it. Early pins get the saves that pay off when the date arrives.

The 45 to 90 day window

Aim to have seasonal content live at least six weeks out, and up to three months for the big holidays. Bigger events pull longer lead times because people plan them further ahead. Mark the dates on a calendar, count back, and set your posting window before the searches start climbing.

Reuse what works each year

Seasons repeat, so your best seasonal pins don't expire. Keep track of which ones performed and bring them back the next year, refreshed with a new image or headline. A short list keeps you organized:

  • note your top seasonal pins after each holiday passes;
  • save the source images so you can remake them;
  • set a reminder to post them again on next year's window.

Do this once and each season gets easier than the last.

Keep last year's images in folders

Reusing seasonal content depends on still having the images when the time comes.
